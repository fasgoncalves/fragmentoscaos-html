🚫 Censored, But Not Silenced: The Truth That Frightens Digital Cowards

Publicado em 2025-08-07 22:51:16

I created a subreddit.
I named it “portugueseCitizens” — a space for free, alert, and non-conforming citizens.
I began sharing reflections from my blog, Fragmentos do Caos, articles that challenge power, denounce corruption, and criticize the rotten state of institutions.

The result? The community was banned. No warning. No explanation. No appeal.

What offends platforms isn’t hate — it’s clarity.

Reddit, like so many other platforms of the “new digital order,” is quick to welcome memes, dances, selfies, and harmless conspiracy theories.
But serious critique? A thoughtful analysis of democracy's decay?
A free voice that doesn’t follow the herd?

That’s dangerous. That must be silenced.

They censor because they are afraid.

Afraid someone might think.
Afraid someone might wake up.
Afraid the mask might slip.

They hide behind “community policies,” but the only policy they serve is that of domesticated conformity.
They speak of freedom — but only for the obedient.

They ban us because they can’t refute us.

But we will not be silenced.

Fragmentos do Caos remains standing, stronger than ever.
This episode only confirms that we are making the right people uncomfortable.
And when that happens, we know we’re on the right path.

They can ban subreddits.
They can hide posts.
They can hide behind cowardly algorithms.

But they cannot stop an idea whose time has come.
And our time is now.

📢 To all readers: stay vigilant.

The control of public discourse is migrating into private hands.
These big platforms have become tools of mental domestication.
And the algorithms deciding what “violates the rules” are written by those who benefit from the people’s ignorance.

So if you are free, think.
If you think, speak.
And if you speak, don’t let them silence you — not even with a sweet voice and promises of “safety.”

Fragmentos do Caos goes on.
More alive, more critical, more urgent.

If they ban us, it’s because we’re pressing the right nerve.
And the truth — that can’t be banned.

Author : Francisco Gonçalves
